“BITS Maharashtra” usually refers to **BIT Maharashtra – Ballarpur Institute of Technology, Chandrapur**, a private engineering and management institute in Maharashtra, and **not** to BITS Pilani.  The profile below is for **Ballarpur Institute of Technology (BIT Maharashtra), Chandrapur**, which is what major education portals label as “BIT Maharashtra”.[1][2]

***

## Overview

Ballarpur Institute of Technology (BIT Maharashtra) is a private, AICTE‑approved institute established in 2009 in Chandrapur district, Maharashtra.  It offers undergraduate, postgraduate and diploma programmes across engineering, management and allied fields, with B.Tech and MBA as its flagship courses.[2][3][1]

Key points:

- Location: Ballarpur/Chandrapur region, Maharashtra, catering mainly to Vidarbha and nearby areas.[1][2]
- Type: Private, self‑financed institute with AICTE approval and multiple engineering branches.[1]
- Focus: Industry‑aligned technical education with emphasis on employability skills and multi‑disciplinary offerings.[4][1]

Students who choose BIT Maharashtra are typically looking for a regional private institute with a wide course mix, moderate fees, and a campus that offers all basic facilities in one place.[2][1]

***

## Courses and Fee

BIT Maharashtra offers 20+ programmes across engineering, management and other streams.[3][1]

### Major programmes

| Level | Stream / Degree (examples) | Notes |
|-------|----------------------------|-------|
| UG    | B.Tech (multiple branches such as Mechanical, Civil, CSE, EEE, etc.) | Flagship offerings in engineering. [1][3] |
| UG    | BBA, BCA and other UG programmes | Vary by year; focused on management/IT. [1] |
| PG    | MBA                         | Key management programme. [1] |
| Diploma | Polytechnic / diploma in engineering | For students entering after Class 10. [1][2] |

Exact branch list can change slightly year to year, so students should check the latest course list on official/brochure pages.[3][1]

### Indicative fee structure

Public data aggregates show BIT Maharashtra as a mid‑range private college on fees.[5][1]

| Programme type | Indicative annual academic fee | Notes |
|----------------|---------------------------------|-------|
| B.Tech         | Typically in the range of ~₹70,000–₹1,00,000 per year (tuition and academic charges). [5][3] |
| MBA            | Higher than some B.Tech branches on per‑year basis; still positioned in typical private‑MBA fee band. [1] |
| Diploma        | Usually lower than B.Tech, adjusted for diploma norms. [1][5] |

- Total cost over the full B.Tech duration will include hostel, mess, exam fees and other charges in addition to tuition.[5]
- Fees may be revised periodically and can differ by category, quota or year of admission, so counselling content should always prompt students to verify the latest fee document.[5][1]

## Admissions

Admissions to BIT Maharashtra are based on a mix of **entrance exams** and **merit**, depending on programme.[3][1]

### Entrance exams and modes

| Programme | Typical admission basis |
|----------|-------------------------|
| B.Tech   | MHT‑CET and/or JEE Main scores, plus state counselling or institute‑level rounds as applicable. [1] |
| MBA      | Exams like CAT, MAT, CMAT, GMAT or other accepted management tests, followed by institute processes. [1] |
| Diploma  | Merit based on qualifying exam (Class 10/12) and state rules. [1][2] |

Key admission points:

- BIT Maharashtra follows Maharashtra state norms for technical education, so seat distribution may include state quota, all‑India quota and management seats.[2][1]
- For engineering, students generally need to register for state counselling (if active for that year) and may also have the option of direct/institute‑level admissions based on available vacancies and regulations.[1]

Counsellors should guide students to:

- Check which entrance scores are accepted in the current year,  
- Confirm minimum eligibility (50% aggregate with Physics/Maths etc. for B.Tech depending on category), and  
- Track institute announcements for spot/late‑admission rounds.[2][1]

## Placements

BIT Maharashtra has a developing but active placement cell and reports modest packages typical of a regional private institute.[1]

### Placement snapshot

| Metric                         | Recent indicative data |
|--------------------------------|------------------------|
| Highest package                | Around ₹10 LPA. [1] |
| Average package                | Around ₹4 LPA. [1] |
| Key recruiting sectors         | Entry‑level roles in engineering services, manufacturing, local industries, and some IT/management roles. [1][2] |

- The institute promotes itself as focusing on industry‑relevant skills and career readiness, which is reflected in its placement messaging.[4][1]
- Students from core branches often target local/regional companies, PSU/competitive exams, or higher studies in addition to on‑campus placements.[2][1]

For StudentHub content, it helps to position BIT Maharashtra placements as **solid but regional**, and not in the same league as top national institutes.

## Rankings

BIT Maharashtra primarily appears in **private portal rankings and comparison lists** rather than in top national rankings.[1][2]

- It is recognised by AICTE and listed with major Indian education portals, which confirms regulatory approval and visibility in the private‑college market.[2][1]
- It does not appear among top NIRF engineering institutes, and its reputation is more local/regional than national.[1][2]

In your counselling write‑ups, you can honestly present it as a **regional private institute with standard approvals**, avoiding overstating rankings.

BIT Maharashtra is described as having “top‑notch infrastructure” with the key facilities expected in a modern private campus.[1]

### Campus and academic facilities

| Facility type   | Details |
|-----------------|---------|
| Classrooms & lecture halls | Modern lecture rooms with basic AV and teaching aids. [1] |
| Laboratories    | Labs supporting major engineering branches. [1][2] |
| Library         | Central library with course books and reference material. [1] |
| Seminar halls   | Spaces for workshops, guest lectures and events. [1] |
| Sports & fitness| Sports complex and fitness centre for students. [1] |

### Hostel and campus life

- Separate hostels for boys and girls are available, with essential facilities for residential students.[2][1]
- Campus also includes common areas, canteen and basic recreational spaces, providing a contained environment for out‑station students.[1]

The overall infrastructure vibe is that of a self‑contained, functional private campus rather than a huge residential university town.

## Faculty

BIT Maharashtra highlights “well‑trained and expert faculty members” across its engineering and management departments.[1]

- Faculty qualifications vary by department, typically including postgraduate degrees in relevant disciplines and industry or academic experience.[2][1]
- The institute explicitly states an aim to equip students with “industry‑relevant skills”, implying that faculty and curriculum are oriented towards practical applications and employability.[1]

Given its size and regional focus, students can usually expect more teacher‑driven instruction with moderate class sizes, rather than heavy research‑driven ecosystems typical of elite institutes.

## Reviews (Student Perspective)

Student reviews collected on major portals show a mixed but generally positive picture consistent with a regional private college.[2][1]

Common positives:

- Good infrastructure relative to local options (labs, library, sports complex, hostel).[1]
- Supportive environment for students from nearby districts who want to stay close to home.[2][1]

Typical concerns:

- Placement outcomes and exposure are more local than national; top‑tier MNC or product‑company presence is limited compared to metro‑city colleges.[2][1]
- As with many private institutes, teaching quality can vary by branch and individual faculty.[1]

These nuances are useful to include in “humanised” content so students have realistic expectations.
